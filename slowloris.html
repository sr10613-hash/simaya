SLOW LORIS

A slow loris is a small mammal that grows up to 18 to 38 cm and weighs around 2.1 kg. Slow lorises are very close to being extinct, as their population has been declined by at least 80% over the last 24 years. Slow lorises are deadly because even with a single bite, you would get venom.

There are a total of 9 slow loris species, these are all of them in order of how common they are (most to least) and where you can find them:

  • Bengal Slow Loris - Northeast India, Bangladesh, Southern China, Myanmar, Thailand, Laos, Cambodia, and Vietnam
  • Sunda/Greater Slow Loris - Peninsular Malaysia, Singapore, and parts of Southern Thailand
  • Pygmy Slow Loris - East of the Mekong River in Vietnam, Laos, Cambodia, and Southern China
  • Borean Slow Loris - Central and Southern Borneo, Indonesia
  • Philipine Slow Loris - The Philippines and Coastal Northern and Eastern Borneo, Indonesia
  • Kayan River Slow Loris - Central and Northern Borneo, Indonesia
  • Sumatran Slow Loris - The island of Sumatra, Indonesia
  • Bangka Slow Loris - The Islands of Bangka and Southwestern Borneo, Indonesia
  • Javan Slow Loris - The Island of Java, Indonesia
